================================================================================
                HORIZON NATIONAL BANK — ESCALATION PROTOCOL
                      Customer Support Call Center
                       Version 4.0 | Effective: Jan 1, 2025
================================================================================

1. PURPOSE
-----------
This protocol establishes clear escalation pathways to ensure that complex,
high-risk, or emotionally sensitive customer interactions are handled by the
appropriate level of authority. Proper escalation protects the customer, the
agent, and the bank from regulatory and reputational risk.

2. ESCALATION TIERS
---------------------

  TIER 1 — AGENT LEVEL
  Agents handle standard inquiries including: account balance questions, basic
  transaction history, password resets, card activation, and low-value dispute
  initiation. Agents may resolve cases independently when:
    - The issue is clearly within documented policy.
    - The required credit or waiver is within agent authority limits (≤ $500).
    - The customer is calm and the path to resolution is clear.

  TIER 2 — TEAM LEAD / SENIOR AGENT
  Escalate to Team Lead when:
    - The customer explicitly requests to speak with a supervisor.
    - A credit or fee waiver between $500–$2,500 is required.
    - The customer has made the same complaint in 3 or more prior contacts within
      60 days without resolution.
    - The customer expresses intent to close their account or switch banks.
    - The agent is unsure of the correct policy application.
    - The interaction has exceeded 20 minutes without a clear resolution path.
  
  TIER 3 — SPECIALIST TEAM / DEPARTMENT ROUTING
  Escalate directly to the appropriate specialist team for:
    - Fraud & Unauthorized Transactions → Fraud Investigation Unit
    - Legal threats or subpoenas → Legal & Compliance Department
    - Suspected AML/suspicious activity → AML Compliance Officer
    - Loan modification or hardship requests → Lending Support Team
    - Premium/Private Banking customers → Dedicated Relationship Manager
    - Regulatory complaints (CFPB, OCC mentions) → Regulatory Affairs Team

  TIER 4 — MANAGEMENT ESCALATION
  Escalate to Branch/Operations Manager when:
    - A credit or adjustment exceeds $2,500.
    - Media involvement or social media threats are mentioned by the customer.
    - The customer identifies as a legal representative or journalist.
    - A Tier 2 resolution attempt has failed and the customer remains unresolved.
    - An agent misconduct complaint is filed during the call.

3. MANDATORY ESCALATION TRIGGERS
----------------------------------
The following scenarios REQUIRE immediate escalation regardless of tier — agents
must NOT attempt to resolve these independently:

  🔴 Customer mentions self-harm, harm to others, or a crisis situation.
       → End the support interaction, escalate to supervisor, and provide the
         National Crisis Hotline: 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline).

  🔴 Customer uses threatening or abusive language toward the agent.
       → Agent may issue one verbal warning, then transfer to supervisor.
         Document the interaction in CRM using the "Hostile Caller" tag.

  🔴 Suspected elder financial abuse or vulnerable customer indicators.
       → Transfer to Fraud Investigation Unit and flag CRM with
         "Vulnerable Customer — Possible Exploitation."

  🔴 Customer claims their account was accessed by law enforcement or a
     government agency.
       → Do not confirm or deny any account actions. Transfer immediately to
         Legal & Compliance.

  🔴 Agent identifies a potential data breach or system anomaly during the call.
       → Alert IT Security on the internal breach hotline (#4911) while keeping
         the customer on hold. Do not disclose the anomaly to the customer.

4. ESCALATION PROCEDURE — STEP BY STEP
-----------------------------------------
  Step 1: Inform the customer clearly and professionally:
          "I want to make sure you receive the best assistance. I'm going to
           connect you with [Team Lead / Specialist] who can resolve this for you."

  Step 2: Brief the receiving party (warm transfer) with:
          - Customer name and verified account number
          - Issue summary (2–3 sentences)
          - Steps already taken
          - Customer's emotional state (e.g., frustrated, distressed, calm)

  Step 3: Log the escalation in CRM before the call ends, including:
          - Escalation tier and reason code
          - Receiving agent/department name or ID
          - Timestamp of escalation

  Step 4: Follow up (if warm transfer was not possible):
          - Provide the customer with a Case Reference Number.
          - Commit to a specific callback timeframe (maximum 4 business hours for
            Tier 2; 1 business day for Tier 3).

5. CHURN RISK ESCALATION (SAVE PROTOCOL)
------------------------------------------
When a customer expresses intent to leave the bank, agents must:
  a) Acknowledge the concern empathetically — never argue or minimize.
  b) Attempt one retention offer from the approved Retention Offers List (CRM).
  c) If the customer remains firm, escalate to Tier 2 for a secondary save attempt.
  d) If the customer still insists, process the request and log a "Churn Risk"
     tag in the CRM for the Retention Analytics Team.

  Do NOT hold a customer's request hostage as a retention tactic. All closure
  or cancellation requests must ultimately be honored.

6. ESCALATION RESPONSE TIME STANDARDS
---------------------------------------
  Tier 2 (Team Lead):        Available within 3 minutes during operating hours.
  Tier 3 (Specialist Team):  Response within 4 business hours; callback if unavailable.
  Tier 4 (Management):       Response within 1 business day; same-day for media/legal.
  Emergency Triggers:        Immediate — no hold time acceptable.

7. AFTER-CALL DOCUMENTATION
-----------------------------
Every escalated call must have a completed CRM note including:
  - Escalation reason code (see Appendix A in the Agent Handbook)
  - Resolution provided or expected resolution date
  - Customer satisfaction indicator (Resolved / Pending / Unresolved)
  - Whether a follow-up commitment was made

Incomplete escalation notes are flagged during weekly QA audits and count
against the agent's compliance score.
