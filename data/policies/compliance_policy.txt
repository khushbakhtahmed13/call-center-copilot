================================================================================
                  HORIZON NATIONAL BANK — COMPLIANCE POLICY
                    Customer Support Agent Standards & Obligations
                       Version 5.1 | Effective: Jan 1, 2025
================================================================================

1. PURPOSE
-----------
This policy ensures that all customer support agents operate in accordance with
applicable banking regulations, data protection laws (including GLBA, CCPA, and
FDCPA), and internal conduct standards. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary
action, fines, or termination.

2. IDENTITY VERIFICATION (KYC — KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER)
-----------------------------------------------------
Before discussing any account details, agents MUST verify the customer's identity
using at least TWO (2) of the following identifiers:

  Primary Identifiers (must use at least one):
    - Full Social Security Number (last 4 digits acceptable for low-risk queries)
    - Government-issued Photo ID number (passport, driver's license)
    - Date of Birth

  Secondary Identifiers (must use at least one):
    - Registered phone number on file
    - Billing address (street + ZIP)
    - Mother's maiden name or security question answer
    - One-time passcode (OTP) sent to registered email or mobile number

  ⚠ IMPORTANT: If a customer fails verification twice, the agent must lock the
  session and advise the customer to visit a branch with valid ID. Do NOT attempt
  a third verification on the same call.

3. DATA PRIVACY & CONFIDENTIALITY
-----------------------------------
  - Agents must never share full account numbers, SSNs, or passwords verbally.
    Mask all sensitive data in notes and screen shares (e.g., XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-4321).
  - Customer data must not be accessed for any purpose unrelated to the active
    support interaction.
  - Sharing customer data with third parties is prohibited without explicit written
    customer consent or a valid legal order.
  - Agents working remotely must use only bank-approved VPN and devices. No
    customer data may be stored locally on personal devices.

4. CALL RECORDING & MONITORING
--------------------------------
  - All calls are recorded for quality assurance and regulatory compliance.
  - Agents must inform customers of call recording at the start of every
    interaction using the approved disclosure: "This call may be recorded for
    quality and training purposes."
  - Agents may not disable, pause, or interfere with call recording systems.

5. FAIR DEBT COLLECTION & COMMUNICATION STANDARDS
---------------------------------------------------
In alignment with the FDCPA and internal conduct policy:
  - Agents must never use threatening, abusive, or misleading language.
  - Agents may not contact customers about past-due accounts outside the hours
    of 8:00 AM – 9:00 PM (customer's local time).
  - Cease-and-desist requests must be escalated to the Collections Compliance
    Team immediately and flagged in the CRM.
  - Agents must never misrepresent the amount owed, consequences of non-payment,
    or the bank's legal authority.

6. ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) OBLIGATIONS
--------------------------------------------
  - Agents who identify suspicious customer behavior during calls (e.g., requests
    to split large transfers, unusual urgency, reluctance to verify identity) must
    file an internal Suspicious Activity Note (SAN) via the CRM.
  - Agents must not tip off the customer that a report has been or may be filed.
  - Refer all SAN situations to the AML Compliance Officer within the same shift.

7. SCRIPT & DISCLOSURE COMPLIANCE
------------------------------------
  - Agents must deliver all mandatory disclosures as scripted and must not
    improvise or omit them, including:
      • Call recording notice
      • Right to dispute notice (during billing discussions)
      • Privacy notice (first contact per calendar year)
  - Deviating from approved scripts during product offers or fee explanations
    is a compliance violation subject to QA scoring penalties.

8. SOCIAL ENGINEERING AWARENESS
---------------------------------
  - Agents must never override identity verification based on emotional pressure
    or urgency expressed by the caller.
  - Requests from callers claiming to be bank executives or law enforcement must
    follow the same verification protocol. Legitimate internal requests are routed
    through secure internal channels, not inbound customer lines.
  - If an agent suspects a social engineering attempt, they must end the call
    professionally and report to the Security & Fraud team immediately.

9. COMPLIANCE TRAINING REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------
All agents must complete:
  - Annual GLBA & Data Privacy certification (due Jan 31 each year)
  - Quarterly AML awareness refresher
  - Monthly QA calibration session with Team Lead

Agents with overdue certifications are restricted from handling Tier 2 and
above cases until training is completed.

10. VIOLATION REPORTING
------------------------
Agents who witness compliance violations by peers must report them via:
  - Internal Ethics Hotline: 1-800-FNB-SAFE (anonymous)
  - Email: compliance@horizonnationalbank.com
  - Direct report to Compliance Officer (Branch or Remote)

Retaliation against whistleblowers is strictly prohibited and grounds for
immediate termination.
